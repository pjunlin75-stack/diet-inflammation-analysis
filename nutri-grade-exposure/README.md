In this exploratory analysis of NHANES 2017–2018 non-alcoholic beverage intake, most beverage items were classified as Nutri-Grade A (69.0%), while 27.0% were classified as Nutri-Grade D. Nutri-Grade B and C items were relatively uncommon.

C/D-grade beverage exposure showed modest but consistent variation across population subgroups. Lower-income participants had higher mean C/D exposure (31.7%) than middle-income (29.5%) and high-income participants (27.4%). C/D exposure also varied by race/ethnicity and age group, with the highest exposure observed among adults aged 66+ (33.3%).

These findings describe exposure patterns only. They do not estimate causal effects of Nutri-Grade labels, behavioural responses to labelling, or metabolic health outcomes.
